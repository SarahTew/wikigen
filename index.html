Wikigen

An analysis of Wikidata about art historians

Scroll Down to explore

Wikidata is one of the largest free and open knowledge databases in the world. Launched in 2012, it now contains over 97 million items, over six million of them people. It appear across the internet—in Wikipedia info boxes, Google About panels, and is a prime source of biographical information on the internet.

While data is good, and open, linked data is better, data in context is best. That's where Wikigen comes in.

Wikigen investigates how Wikidata describes art historians and how these descriptions compare across genders.

But that's only part of the story...

Even if we close the gap and add more female art historians to Wikidata, are our descriptions of them fair or gender-biased?

Wikigen asks: How do Wikidata descriptions of male and female art historians compare?

Granularity

How detailed is the portrait Wikidata paints?

  • Wikidata describes the average male art historian in 102 properties but the average female art historian in 72.
  • Female art historians are described with 30% fewer properties than male art historians.
  • Fewer properties means fewer pixels and a blurrier portrait of the historian.

Authority Linking

How does Wikidata connect art historians to other representations of themselves?

VIAF

VIAF combines multiple name authorities from 60 institutions into a single id.

How many art historians does Wikidata link to a VIAF?

  • VIAF is the most popular external ID.
  • 98% of male art historians have a VIAF declaration.
  • 92% of female art historians have a VIAF declaration.

All Authority Linking

There's more to authorities than just VIAF.

How many total external authorities does Wikidata declare?

  • The average female art historian is linked to 9 external authorities.
  • The average male art historian is linked to 13 external authorities.
  • Female art historians are less linked to other authoritative representations of themselves.

Personal Relationships

How often does Wikidata describe art historians' family relationships?
  • 10% of female art historians have at least one personal relationship declared.
  • 13% of male art historians have at least one personal relationship declared.
What does Wikidata say about art historians with declared personal relationships?
  • Male art historians' personal relationships are more likely to be declared than female art historians'.
  • The most common personal relationship property for male art historians was father.
  • The most common personal relationship property for female art historians was spouse.
  • 59% of female art historians have a spouse declared while only 34% of male art historians do.

Professional Relationships

How often does Wikidata describe art historians' professional relationships?
  • Professional relationships are less declared than personal relationships for both genders but the gap is larger for female art historians.
  • Compared to personal relationships, men's professional relationships are 47% less likely to be declared and women's are 65% less likely.
How does Wikidata describe art historians' professional relationships?
  • Relationships with teachers (doctoral advisors and student of) are more likely to be declared than those with students.
  • Doctoral advisors are an anomaly: 49% of female art historians have a doctoral advisor vs 20% of male art historians.
  • Use of 'student of' is roughly equal between men and woman but the gap between doctoral advisor is 29 percentage points.

So what does this all mean?

A person's Wikidata is a portrait of them.

Wikidata contributors are the artist. The properties are the palette. Each declaration is a brushstroke.

The final portrait is a likeness, informed by factors well beyond the reality of the sitter.

Wikidata paints the average male and female art historians in different ways.

Understanding that contribution to the portrait helps us better understand the person it depicts and history-making itself.
